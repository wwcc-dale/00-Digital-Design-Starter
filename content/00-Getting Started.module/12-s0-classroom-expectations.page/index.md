# Classroom Expectations

The quiz that follows this page draws directly from what is written here.
Read carefully. Take notes if it helps. You are allowed to look back at this
page during the quiz.

---

## Pace

Every student is expected to complete **one credit per week**. A credit is
five sessions, each about three hours of focused work.

All courses are divided into one-credit modules so you always know exactly
what you should be finishing each week. If you are not on pace, talk to
your instructor before the end of the week.

---

## Technology and Internet Use

Lab computers have internet access. That access is for course-related work
only.

**Allowed:**
- Canvas course materials
- Software tutorials that are part of your coursework
- Program software (Photoshop, Illustrator, VS Code, etc.)

**Not allowed:**
- Social media and messaging apps
- Videos, music, or entertainment not related to your coursework
- Any site or app that is not directly part of what you are working on

**Consequences:**

| [light] | |
|---|---|
| First time | Verbal warning from your instructor |
| Second time | Directed to return to your housing unit for that session |

---

## Asking for Help

You are expected to ask for help when you are stuck. Your instructor and any
teaching assistants are there to help you. If you have been stuck for more
than a few minutes, raise your hand or catch your instructor's eye. Do not
wait and hope the confusion goes away on its own.

---

## Helping Other Students

You may help classmates if they ask and you are not in the middle of something
important. Help means explaining — walking them through how something works,
pointing to the instruction they need, asking questions that guide them.

Help does **not** mean doing the work for them. Completing another student's
assignment for them is not allowed.

---

## One Session Per Day

Ordinarily, students may attend **only one session per day**.

The one exception: if a student is close to finishing a one-year certificate
or the AAS degree and their release timeline is tight, the instructor may
request enrollment in both daily sessions. This requires an instructor request
and is not guaranteed.

If this situation applies to you, talk to your instructor.

---

## Graded Work

**Ungraded review activities** are for your learning only. They do not count
toward your grade. Use them honestly — they tell you whether you understood
the material.

**Graded quizzes** are tied to specific points in the course: end of each
credit module, midterm, and final. They confirm you have mastered enough
to move forward.

**Assignment submissions** must show your own work. All assignments have
a rubric that tells you exactly how grading works.

## Saving and File Management

Save your work constantly using Ctrl+S (Windows) or Cmd+S (Mac). Files that
are not saved can disappear if the computer restarts or loses power.

Always save to your class folder on the shared drive — not just to the desktop
or local hard drive.

---

## Withdrawal

You may withdraw from the program with no questions asked within your **first
week — up to five sessions**.

After five sessions, you may still request a withdrawal, but a program review
is likely before a decision is made.
