## The Three Skill Areas — And Their Jobs

- flow-tabs
### Graphic Design

Graphic designers create the visual materials that businesses use every day — logos, ads, packaging, social media graphics, layouts, printed publications, and more.

**Common job titles:**

- Graphic Designer
- Visual Designer
- Marketing Designer
- Branding Specialist
- Advertising Designer
- Freelance Designer

**Who hires graphic designers:**
Print shops, marketing agencies, in-house design teams at businesses,
nonprofits, government agencies, publishers, and self-employed clients.

**The numbers in Washington State:**

| [light] | |
|---|---|
| Workers in WA | ~4,928 |
| Annual job openings in WA | ~842 |
| WA average annual wage | $73,100 |
| WA mean wage (BLS) | $75,680 |
| Seattle area mean wage | $80,870 |
| National median wage | $58,910 |
| Projected job growth (2024–2034) | 2% — slower than average |

Washington is the **4th highest-paying state** for graphic designers nationally — wages here run about 17% above the national mean.[^gd-bls][^gd-cb]

**A word about AI and graphic design:**

This is worth being honest about. The World Economic Forum's 2025 Future of Jobs Report lists graphic design among the occupations most likely to see disruption from AI tools. Software like Midjourney, Adobe's Generative Fill, and similar tools are automating routine production tasks — resizing, background removal, simple layout work — that once required significant designer time.[^gd-wef]

This does not mean graphic design is going away. It means the job is shifting. AI handles production; designers handle judgment — brand strategy, client collaboration, conceptual problem-solving. The same WEF report identifies **UI/UX design as the 8th fastest-growing job category** through 2030. Creative thinking ranked fourth among skills expected to *grow* in importance by 2030, not decline.[^gd-figma]

Designers who add UI/UX skills, motion graphics, and AI tool fluency to their toolkit are well-positioned. That is exactly what this program builds toward.

### Web Development

Web developers build the websites and applications people use every day.
Front-end developers handle what you see. Back-end developers handle the
databases and logic behind the scenes. Full-stack developers do both.

**Common job titles:**

- Front-End Web Developer
- Full-Stack Developer
- Web Designer
- UI Developer
- Junior Software Developer
- Web Application Developer

**Who hires web developers:**
Tech companies, marketing agencies, small businesses building their own sites,
government agencies, schools, hospitals, and startups.

**The numbers in Washington State:**

| [light] | |
|---|---|
| Web developers (SOC 15-1254) employed in WA | ~4,820–5,893 |
| Web & digital interface designers (SOC 15-1255) in WA | ~8,250 |
| Annual openings in WA (web developers) | ~2,534 |
| WA mean wage — web developers | $120,910 |
| WA mean wage — digital interface designers | $119,920 |
| Seattle area mean — web developers | $125,040 |
| Seattle area mean — digital interface designers | $128,650 |
| National median — web developers | $84,960 |
| National median — digital interface designers | $98,540 |
| Projected growth (2024–2034) | 7% — much faster than average |

Washington has the **#1 concentration of web developer jobs** in the country — 2.45 times the national average. For digital interface designers, the concentration is even higher: 3.23 times the national average, the highest of any state. The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metro area alone accounts for over 6,400 digital interface designer jobs.[^web-bls][^web-cb]

**Full-stack vs. front-end — and why it matters:**

The program teaches full-stack web development, and that is a deliberate choice. Job postings for front-end-only roles dropped 24% year-over-year in 2025, while demand for full-stack developers remained stronger. AI tools are increasingly handling routine UI implementation tasks — which means pure front-end specialization is the most AI-vulnerable slice of web development. Full-stack developers — who can work across the entire pipeline — have a more durable skill profile and continue to command strong salaries.[^fsfe]

That said, strong front-end skills are a core part of being a good full-stack developer. The goal is to be versatile, not to avoid the front end.

**A word about AI and web development:**

The 2025 Stack Overflow Developer Survey found 84% of developers now use or plan to use AI tools. AI is reshaping how code gets written — but web development employment is still projected to grow 7% through 2034. Developers who can orchestrate, validate, and debug AI-generated code are becoming more valuable, not less. Engineers with strong AI skills command an 18% salary premium.[^web-ai]

### Combining Both
Many of the best-paying and most interesting jobs sit at the crossroads of
design and development. These roles need people who can think visually
*and* build technically.

**Job titles at the crossroads:**

- UX/UI Designer
- Creative Technologist
- Digital Content Creator
- Interactive Designer
- Product Designer

The World Economic Forum specifically calls out UI/UX design as one of the fastest-growing job categories through 2030 — and these roles require exactly the combination of skills this program builds: visual design thinking plus the ability to build on the web.[^gd-wef]

### Rural Washington

Most of the employment data above is dominated by the Seattle metro area. That is where the jobs are concentrated. But it is not the whole picture for students who will release to rural areas and small towns.

**Web development is remote-friendly by nature.** The field is well-suited to distributed teams, and the expansion of remote hiring means that it's possible for strong web developers in rural Washington to access Seattle-level salaries without relocating. Web developer roles in rural Washington show mean wages around $95,000 even for the relatively small number of locally-based positions — and remote openings regularly list salaries in the $120,000+ range.[^web-bls]

**Graphic design is increasingly remote too.** While the field is more location-dependent than web development, strong portfolios travel. Designers who develop digital and web skills (which this program builds) have the most access to remote-friendly work.

Spokane represents the closest regional tech market, with strong demand for full-stack developers with rewarding salaries.[^web-indeed]

## Why These Skills Travel Well

One of the most practical things about design and web skills is that they are
**portable**. You can work for an employer, freelance on your own, or do both.
You are not locked into one industry or one location.

A graphic designer can work for a print shop today, a marketing agency next
year, and freelance for clients the year after that — building a reputation
and a client list over time.

A web developer can work remotely for companies across the country. Many
web development jobs do not require you to be in a specific city.

## What "Sustainable and Profitable" Actually Means

This program's goal is to prepare you for a career that is:

**Sustainable** — Skills you can keep using and building on for years.
Design and development tools change, but the fundamentals stay the same.
Once you know how to think like a designer or a developer, you can adapt
to new tools — including AI tools.

**Profitable** — These are not minimum-wage jobs. Washington State graphic designers earn a mean wage of $75,680. Web developers and digital interface designers in Washington earn mean wages above $119,000. Experienced full-stack developers in the Seattle metro regularly earn $125,000–$160,000+. Even in Eastern Washington, locally-based roles show mean wages around $95,000, and remote access to metro-level salaries is real.[^web-bls][^gd-bls]

**Rewarding** — You build things you can see. You solve real problems for
real people. You create work you can be proud of and show to others.

## What Employers Look for

Beyond technical skills, employers consistently say they hire people who:

- Can complete projects on time without constant supervision
- Contribute to a team and collaborate well with other team members
- Communicate clearly about what they are doing and why
- Listen to feedback, accept constructive criticism and use it to improve their work
- Show initiative — they do not wait to be told every next step
- Have a portfolio of real work they can point to

This program is designed to develop the student's level of professionalism by teaching all of the skills above alongside the technical aptitudes. The pacing expectations, the project assignments, and the capstone projects are all preparing you for how professional work actually functions.

## A Note on the Capstone Projects

Every one-year certificate and the AAS degree end with a **capstone project**.
This is a major piece of work you build from start to finish — a real website,
a complete brand identity, or a working web application.

Capstones matter because they are a real demonstration of your skills you can show prospective employers.

- A certificate without a portfolio project is credentials on paper.
- A certificate with a strong capstone is proof you can do the work.
