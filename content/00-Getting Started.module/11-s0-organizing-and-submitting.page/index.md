# Organizing and Submitting Your Work

## The Weekly Rhythm

This program runs on a clear weekly cycle. Every student follows the same pattern, regardless of which course or credit module they are currently in.

- steps: indigo
- **Work through the week** — complete your credit module sessions across the week, one credit (five sessions) by Friday is the expectation for every student
- **Submit on Friday** — all completed credit work is due by the end of class on Friday
- **Monday check-in** — your instructor will follow up with you individually on Monday to review your progress and build a work plan for the week ahead

The Friday submission deadline and Monday check-in are not bureaucratic formalities. They are the mechanism that keeps small pace problems from becoming large ones. A student who is half a credit behind on Monday can recover. A student who has been quietly slipping for three weeks cannot.

---

## The Monday Work Plan

Every Monday your instructor will sit down with you briefly — not to evaluate you, but to plan with you. Together you will look at where you finished last week and map out what you need to complete by Friday.

This conversation is short. It might be five minutes. But it means you always know exactly what you are working toward and your instructor always knows where you are. If something came up last week that slowed you down, Monday is the time to surface it.

> alert: tip
>
> Come to your Monday check-in knowing where you left off. Have Canvas open and be ready to say which session or assignment you are on. The more prepared you are, the faster and more useful the conversation.

## Your Journal

Every student in this program keeps a journal. It is not a diary — it is a working document you build throughout each course and submit at the end for a grading bonus. It has two purposes: planning your week and capturing what you take away from Documentary Thursday.

**Your journal lives in a Word document that you create and make your own.** There is no single right way for it to look — the cover page is yours to design.

---

### Setting It Up

- steps: indigo
- **Create a new Word document** and save it to your class folder as `Lastname_Firstname_Journal.docx`
- **Design your cover page** — include your name, the course name, and an image that represents you or your work. This is your document. Make it look like something you are proud of.
- **Add your first Monday entry** using the format described below
- **Bring it to every class** — you will add to it every Monday morning and every Thursday after the documentary

---

### The Two Entry Types

**Monday — Weekly Plan** *(written first thing Monday morning)*

Each Monday entry answers two questions:

1. What did I complete last week?
2. What will I accomplish this week?

Keep it specific. "I will finish Credit 2" is not a plan. "I will complete sessions 3, 4, and 5 of Credit 2 and submit by Friday" is a plan.

---

**Thursday — Documentary Reflection** *(written after the screening)*

Each Thursday entry captures what you took from the film. A strong entry typically covers:

- Something that surprised you, challenged your thinking, or that you want to remember
- A connection to your own work, goals, or experience
- Your honest reaction — agreement, disagreement, or a question the film left you with

You may also include an image of a project you completed that week that you are proud of. This is optional but encouraged — over time your journal becomes a record of your growth.

---

> alert: info
>
> Your journal is submitted at the end of each course as part of your final grade. Rubric details and submission instructions are included in each course. For now, just get it set up and start your first entry on Monday.
